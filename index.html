Is a trip to Minnesota on your list?

Natural Beauty: Known as the "Land of 10,000 Lakes," Minnesota is famous for its scenic waterways, forests, and state parks, including the Boundary Waters Canoe Area and Itasca State Park.

Vibrant Cities: Explore the Twin Cities of Minneapolis and Saint Paul, where you’ll find cultural landmarks such as the Walker Art Center, the Guthrie Theater, and the Minnesota State Capitol.

Outdoor Adventures: From hiking and biking to kayaking and fishing, Minnesota offers endless outdoor activities for nature enthusiasts and families alike.

Rich History: Discover Minnesota's Native American heritage at the Mille Lacs Indian Museum and its Scandinavian roots at the American Swedish Institute in Minneapolis.

Underrated Food Scene: Minnesota's culinary landscape includes iconic dishes like Juicy Lucy burgers and tater tot hotdish, as well as thriving farm-to-table and global cuisine options.

What to Wear:

The Minnesota State Fair is a lively event that spans across warm summer days. Wear light, comfortable clothing and walking shoes to navigate the fairgrounds with ease.

Summer: Opt for t-shirts, shorts, or casual dresses. Don’t forget sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat for sun protection.

Regardless of the season, comfortable shoes are essential for walking around the event spaces and fairgrounds.

Reservations:

While the fair itself does not require reservations, planning ahead for nearby attractions or restaurants is recommended:

  • Revival: A popular spot for fried chicken and southern comfort food in Minneapolis.
  • The Bachelor Farmer: Renowned for its Nordic-inspired cuisine.
  • Mickey’s Diner: A historic spot for a classic diner experience.
